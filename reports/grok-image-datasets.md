**Executive summary**  
For embedding-geometry experiments (centroid distances, cluster compactness, class overlaps, raw-image vs. segmented-object comparisons, and cross-modality alignment with text sentiment/emotion geometry), the most useful image datasets are those with **meaningful, reasonably clean affective labels** on whole scenes (not just faces), sufficient scale (ideally >10k images) for stable statistics, and diversity that avoids trivial visual confounds (e.g., domain-specific semantics or artistic style).  

Pure face-only datasets (FER2013, AffectNet) or tiny/highly-controlled psychology sets (IAPS subsets, GAPED, ArtPhoto, Abstract ~<1k) are poorly suited despite fame, as clusters collapse on faces or low-level aesthetics rather than evoked sentiment. Highly domain-biased sets (disaster images) introduce semantic confounds. The strongest candidates are crowdsourced social-media or in-the-wild sets with multiclass emotions (Mikels/Plutchik models) or dimensional valence-arousal (V/A) that can be discretized or analyzed directly. EmoSet is the clear winner for scale + rich attributes that enable dissecting whether geometry is driven by true affect vs. objects/scenes. FI follows closely. FindingEmo adds continuous V/A on wild images. Smaller but unique sets like EmotionROI enable direct image-vs-region geometry tests.

**Detailed notes on each strong candidate**  
- **EmoSet (rank 1)**: Best overall match. Scale supports reliable cluster compactness and centroid-distance metrics even in high-dimensional embedding spaces. Rich attributes are a major advantage—you can stratify analyses (e.g., “do positive/negative clusters separate better when controlling for object class or scene type?”) or directly compare geometry of raw images vs. object-centric subsets. Diversity (social + artistic) reduces trivial confounds better than single-domain sets. Labels are evoked emotions (not just depicted), aligning well with text sentiment geometry experiments. Minor weakness: non-commercial license only.  
- **FI (rank 2)**: Very close second—essentially a smaller, simpler version of EmoSet with the same 8-class taxonomy and solid crowdsourced quality. Excellent for direct replication/comparison with prior visual-sentiment literature. Lacks attributes, so harder to dissect confounds, but still large enough for geometry work.  
- **FindingEmo (rank 3)**: Strong for dimensional geometry (V/A space naturally lends itself to distance/overlap metrics) and for “in-the-wild” diversity beyond posed or artistic images. Plutchik labels enable finer affect and easy discretization (e.g., high/low valence for polarity). Higher-order social scenes are a plus for realism. Access is slightly less convenient (URLs + scraping), but annotations are high-quality (Prolific).  
- **Emotion6/EmotionROI (rank 4)**: The only dataset with explicit region-level emotion data, making it uniquely suited for the “raw image vs. segmented object” geometry comparison the research goal explicitly mentions. Small size is the main drawback—clusters/centroids will be noisier and overlap statistics less reliable. Still valuable as a complementary “micro” dataset.  
- **Image-Sentiment (Simula, rank 5)**: Excellent multi-annotator quality and direct polarity + influence-factor questions that help probe object-level contributions. Continuous scales are useful. However, the disaster domain is a significant confound: visual cues (destruction, rescue, etc.) are tightly coupled with negative sentiment, likely inflating semantic-over-affect overlap in embeddings. Best used as a robustness check rather than primary dataset.

**Rejected / weak candidates and why**  
- **Small classics (ArtPhoto ~806, Abstract ~280, GAPED ~730, OASIS ~900)**: Insufficient scale for stable geometry statistics; artistic/controlled stimuli introduce strong low-level (color/composition) confounds unrelated to evoked sentiment.  
- **Face-centric (FER2013, AffectNet, CK+)**: Clusters collapse on facial features rather than holistic image sentiment/affect—poor for the stated goal.  
- **VSO / SentiBank Flickr (~500k raw but indirect)**: ANP (adjective-noun pair) detectors are mid-level concepts; per-image sentiment is derived rather than direct. Too indirect for clean cluster analysis.  
- **Pure polarity Twitter/Crowdflower sets (~1k–15k)**: Often binary or 5-level polarity; many dead links or low-quality images now; smaller effective size and high domain variance.  
- **Aesthetic/preference datasets (e.g., AVA)**: Focus on beauty/quality, not sentiment/affect—only marginally relevant.  
- **IAPS subsets**: High-quality normative V/A but restricted access/licensing and small scale; psychology-controlled rather than natural images.  

**Final recommendation**  
**Top 3 datasets**:  
1. **EmoSet** — Start here. Its combination of scale (118k), balanced 8-class labels, and rich object/scene attributes makes it the single best resource for rigorous centroid, cluster-compactness, and overlap experiments while allowing controlled analyses of confounds.  
2. **FI** — Immediate follow-up or comparison set; nearly identical taxonomy but smaller and simpler—perfect for validating geometric findings on an independent but closely related corpus.  
3. **FindingEmo** — Add for dimensional (V/A) geometry and wild-scene diversity; enables direct comparison of discrete vs. continuous affect spaces and easy cross-modality alignment with text V/A datasets.  

Together these three provide scale, variety, and complementary label types (multiclass + dimensional) while minimizing the worst confounds. Use EmotionROI as a targeted supplement for object-level segmentation experiments and Simula Image-Sentiment only for polarity-focused robustness checks. This shortlist directly supports the research objective without chasing benchmark accuracy. All are publicly downloadable or scrapable for research use.
